# 60 Decibels: Comprehensive Company Playbook

## Executive Summary
60 Decibels (60dB) is an end-to-end, tech-enabled social impact measurement company. Their mission is to help organizations—including corporations, impact investors, NGOs, and foundations—understand the true social impact of their work by directly collecting and analyzing data from the people they serve (customers, farmers, employees, and beneficiaries). 60dB spun out of Acumen in 2019 to scale their proprietary "Lean Data" approach and establish it as the global standard for impact measurement.

## Core Value Proposition
60 Decibels solves the classic problem of impact measurement: traditional evaluations are often too complex, slow, and expensive to be scalable, or too simple to be genuinely useful. 60dB provides a "Goldilocks approach": high-quality, actionable, benchmarked social performance data gathered directly from real human beings. 

*   **Data Built by Listening:** They bypass top-down assumptions and ask the end-users themselves via phone, SMS, and other remote technologies, ensuring the measurement is rooted in what matters most to the beneficiaries' lives.
*   **Speedy & Standardized:** Their foundation relies on 15-minute, standardized surveys designed for repeatability, comparability, and speed.
*   **Benchmarked Performance:** They provide clients with benchmarks drawn from a massive global database of impact results, allowing organizations to compare their impact performance against peers in their specific sectors.

## Who They Serve (Target Audience)
60dB tailors its solutions to three main categories of organizations:
1.  **Corporations:** Helping businesses integrate social performance into their core operations, improve their supply chains, and build lasting relationships with workers and micro-entrepreneurs.
2.  **Investors & Funders:** Giving impact investors, private equity firms, and philanthropies actionable data to vet prospective companies, track portfolio performance, and make data-driven investment decisions.
3.  **NGOs & Social Enterprises:** Enabling operational teams to listen continuously to their beneficiaries, iterate on program design, and prove their impact to stakeholders and donors.

## Core Offerings & Products
*   **Lean Data Measurement:** Their flagship service. Deploying researchers across 80+ countries to speak directly with customers in their local languages.
*   **60dB Signal:** A tool aimed at funders and investors to help them act faster, vet smarter, and find companies aligned with their investment criteria using verified customer data. 
*   **Sector Benchmarks & Indices:** Generating massive, sector-wide reports that establish the baseline for impact. Examples include:
    *   **The Microfinance (MFI) Index:** Evaluating the actual client impact of financial service providers globally.
    *   **The Farmer Thriving Index:** Tracking the well-being and resilience of smallholder farmers.

## Areas of Expertise
60 Decibels maps its research and methodologies across numerous essential impact themes:
*   **Agriculture:** Smallholder farmer resilience, chatbot/AI usage among farmers, etc.
*   **Climate Resilience:** How vulnerable populations are adapting to climate shocks.
*   **Education:** Outcomes for learners globally.
*   **Financial Inclusion:** Entrepreneur-friendly inclusive finance and lending impacts.
*   **Gender:** Focusing heavily on gender equity and empowering women.
*   **Health & Disability:** Improving healthcare access and outcomes.
*   **Micro & Small Businesses:** Supporting MSMEs, primarily in emerging markets (e.g., LatAm, Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia).
*   **Off-Grid Energy:** Evaluating the impact of clean energy access for unconnected populations.
*   **Quality Jobs:** Workforce development and employment standards.
*   **Supply Chains:** Ethical sourcing, supporting rural workers, and transparency.

## The Team & Global Presence
60dB operates with a large, diverse, and globally distributed team of researchers, data scientists, client success managers, and strategists. Key hubs and operational bases include the United States, Europe, India, Kenya, Latin America, and West Africa. This local footprint is vital to their survey methodology, enabling them to converse with beneficiaries in native dialects over the phone.
